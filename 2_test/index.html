5th of November 2019
A world premiere in pediatric oncology

Developed by Bite Size Entertainment with Dr. Catherine Goudie, MIPOGG is a mobile application that identifies cancer predisposition syndromes in pediatric oncology.

A true decision support tool, MIPOGG (for McGill Interactive Pediatric OncoGenetic Guidelines)provides access to simple, tumor-specific algorithms that allow the clinician to determine whether a child requires genetic evaluation. 

Because no child should die of cancer.

Oncologists and health professionals who follow children and adolescents with cancer will now have a unique opportunity to more accurately understand the predisposition syndromes to cancer. An increasingly complex task as our knowledge of genetic cancers grows rapidly.

A worldwide echo.

The launch of MIPOGG took place in the context of SIOP 2019, the International Congress of Pediatric Oncology, held in Lyon, France. Already relying on collaborators in more than 6 countries, the evolution of MIPOGG will go through its ability to collect more data and include new predictive tools using AI. The goal is to better understand cancer susceptibility syndromes and thus save more lives. 

The application is available on the PLAY store as well as on the APP store.

24th of November 2016
6 Key Insights From the EY Strategic Growth Forum

USA’s most prestigious gathering of high-growth, market-leading companies, was held in beautiful Palm Spring last week. It featured a slew of the country’s finest entrepreneurs and CEOs.

We were treated with 4 full days of conferences and from family business to M&A forecast, the hot topic that seemed to resonate with most of the speakers was: ‘’Purpose-driven businesses‘’. Needless to say, this sort of value needs to become a reality that transcends to the core of any business these days.

Having just jumped off the plane, I thought I’d run by the 6 best insights that stuck with me since this year’s EY Strategic Growth Forum:

1.Learn From Your History

President & CEO of Vitamix Jodi L. Berg gave a passionate speech and exposed Vitamix’s corporate culture, in regards to the power of being aligned on strong values which, according to her, enables her team to dream and feel safe while doing it. Showcasing the history behind the company and how it thrived for more than 85 years, she dug out 8 critical lessons that her family had learned over time. Lessons she makes sure everyone in the business gets exposed to.

She also shares her favorite acronym; D.A.N.N.C.E (No, it’s not a typo), which means serious business: 

1.     Destiny (Always start with the end in mind)

2.     Align people around you (Same direction)

3.     Network of people around you to get help. (Enables you to benchmark and learn about the best practices.)

4.     Net (People willing to catch you if you fall)

5.     Care for yourself first

6.     Embrace the process

2.What's your contribution to the human family? 

‘’If you figure out how you can be most useful and maximize how you help others, you will make money” says award-winning actor Will Smith. 

Funny and natural, he encourages people to stay true to themselves by embracing their differences. In front of the largest crowd of the conference, he discussed planning, which he considers to be 80% of any job.

3.Surround Yourself With Positive Young People

Energetic and charismatic, Shark Tank star Barbara Corcoran explained that she fuels her vitality by staying surrounded by young people. As the average age of her team is 28 years old, she prides herself in the fun she has doing what she loves. When talking about the show, she took the time to evaluate businesses based on the grit of their entrepreneurs, saying she couldn’t care less about the idea, as long as the head of the company has the ability to sell.

4.It’s Going to Be All Right

The recent election was also at the forefront of the four-day conference. Both Karl Rove and Howard Dean engaged in giving reassuring speeches to the floor. On his end,EY TAS leader Bill Casey revealed that 75% of the US businesses surveyed by EY were more than willing to perform M&A transactions in 2017. Isaac Larian from MGA Entertainment, who met Trump several times, was also convinced that the newly elected president wouldn’t shake things as much as the general public perceived it. Needless to say, the next few months will be interesting…

5.Be the Leader You Wish You Had

Prolific author Simon Sinek discussed the principle behind every successful person and business. A simple and powerful model on leadership and how you should start with the main question: Why? Through his stage presence, Sinek inspired leaders to take action by becoming the leader they wished they had earlier in their career.

6.Manage With Compassion  

2011 Entrepreneur of the Year Winner, Linkedin’s CEO Jeff Weiner, while being interviewed by EY’s CEO Mark Weinberger, spoke about his guiding philosophy of being compassionate towards people. ‘’Understand where they are coming from and work for a positive outcome“ says Weiner, right before presenting his 5 keys to happiness:

1.     Live in the moment

2.     It’s better to be loved than right

3.     Be a spectator of your own thoughts (projection helps empathy)

4.     Be grateful of one thing everyday

5.     Help those around you

This was all coming from a man who recently sold his company for 26 billion dollars to Microsoft. He also mentioned that the relationship he had with the tech giant was based on a similar visions and values. He then advised on clarifying your understanding of what you wish to accomplish and, most importantly, staying focused in the process.

For Weiner (and many other speakers), artificial intelligence and conversational computing represent the future of computing. While keeping an open eye on that matter, he reminded the audience of capital importance of optimizing your passion and skills as well and reinforcing your core. The CEO ended his session with perhaps one of the most powerful statements of the conference. When asked about how he stayed the same person throughout his career, he responded: ‘’Being down to earth shouldn’t be a compliment, but an expectation“.

To say that my time at this year’s EY Forum was enlightening is a total understatement. From beginning to end, I felt compelled and energized, leaving the golden state with a powerfully optimistic outlook on things to come.

Once again, I’d like to congratulate the organizers, speakers and all Entrepreneur of the Year winners on behalf of myself and the entire Bite Size Entertainment team

- JF DuRocher
